How pRb Mutations Disrupt Mitosis and Cause Cancer

Mutations in the retinoblastoma protein (pRb) gene impair the loading of the condensin II complex, which is essential for proper chromosome condensation during mitosis. This leads to less compact chromosomes, increasing susceptibility to DNA breaks during prolonged mitosis. These defects contribute to genomic instability, promoting tumor formation and cancer aggressiveness, as seen in mouse models. This connection highlights how mitotic errors can drive cancer development beyond simple chromosome missegregation.
